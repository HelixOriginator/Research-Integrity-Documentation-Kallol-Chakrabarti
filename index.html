A Resource for Independent Researchers
How Institutions Can Fail to Categorize High-Velocity Independent Research

This case documents a critical failure mode in how global research repositories handle independent, high-output scholars. The researcher produced 261 DOI-registered works across three distinct functional categories — Dissemination, Infrastructure, and Validation — as formally defined by the Kallol Taxonomy of Research DOIs.

When platforms built for traditional academic output encounter high-velocity independent research, their automated systems may misclassify legitimate intellectual production. This is documented here not as a complaint — but as a case study for the global research community on how institutions can fail, and how researchers can protect themselves.

What Every Independent Researcher Should Know
Your DOI is permanent. It exists in DataCite, CrossRef, and ORCID registries regardless of any platform action. No repository can delete a registered DOI.
Document everything immediately. Timestamped public archives on GitHub are free, permanent, and carry legal and reputational weight.
Build platform independence. Host your own files. Own your own infrastructure. No single platform should be the sole access point to your work.
A "final decision" is not always final. Institutional systems make errors. Calm, documented, professional persistence is more powerful than any complaint.
Your recognition record is your strongest asset. Government acknowledgements, peer verifications, and institutional citations cannot be deleted by any platform.
Speed of response matters. This researcher went from deletion to a fully independent self-hosted repository in 7 days — without losing a single DOI.
